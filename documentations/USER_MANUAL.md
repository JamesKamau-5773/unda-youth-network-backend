## üéØ What is UNDA Youth Network?

**UNDA Youth Network** is a digital platform designed to support youth mental health initiatives through a peer prevention advocate network. It's a comprehensive system that helps:

- **Young people (Prevention Advocates)** access mental health support, participate in events, apply for funding, and track their personal development
- **Supervisors** manage and support their assigned prevention advocates, monitor wellbeing, and submit performance reports
- **Administrators** oversee the entire program, manage users, track compliance, and generate analytics

### Why UNDA?

Think of UNDA as your **all-in-one youth support hub** that brings together:
- ‚úÖ Mental health screening and support
- ‚úÖ Daily motivational affirmations
- ‚úÖ Community events and workshops
- ‚úÖ Seed funding for youth projects
- ‚úÖ Educational resources through blogs and podcasts
- ‚úÖ Performance tracking and reporting
- ‚úÖ Secure communication and data management

## üîê Login & Account Security

### How to Log In

1. Go to the UNDA website
2. Enter your **email** or **username**
3. Enter your **password**
4. Click **"Login"**

### Password Requirements

Your password must:
- Be at least 8 characters long
- Contain at least one UPPERCASE letter
- Contain at least one lowercase letter
- Contain at least one number (0-9)
- Contain at least one special character (!@#$%^&*)

**Example of a strong password:** Unda2026!Safe

### Account Lockout Protection

For your security, your account will be **locked for 30 minutes** after **7 failed login attempts**. This prevents unauthorized access to your account.

If locked out:
1. Wait 30 minutes
2. Try logging in again
3. If you've forgotten your password, contact your supervisor or admin

### Forgot Your Password?

**Currently:** Contact your supervisor or administrator to reset your password.

**Coming Soon:** Self-service password reset via email.

### Security Tips

‚úÖ **DO:**
- Keep your password private
- Use a unique password (don't reuse passwords from other sites)
- Log out when using shared computers
- Contact admin if you notice suspicious activity

‚ùå **DON'T:**
- Share your password with anyone
- Write your password down where others can see it
- Use simple passwords like "password123"
- Leave your account logged in on public computers

## üéì Prevention Advocate Guide

As a Prevention Advocate, your dashboard is your personal hub for mental health support, personal development, and community engagement.

### Your Prevention Advocate Dashboard

When you log in, you'll see:

#### üìä Quick Stats
- Your completion rate for assessments
- Upcoming events you're registered for
- Recent affirmations
- Your participation history

#### üß† Mental Health Assessments

**What are they?**
Standardized questionnaires (PHQ-9, GAD-7) that help assess your mental health and wellbeing.

**How to complete an assessment:**

1. Click **"Take Assessment"** from your dashboard
2. Choose the assessment type:
   - **PHQ-9** (Depression screening - 9 questions)
   - **GAD-7** (Anxiety screening - 7 questions)
   - **PHQ-2** (Quick depression check - 2 questions)
   - **GAD-2** (Quick anxiety check - 2 questions)
3. Answer each question honestly
4. Submit the assessment
5. View your results and personalized recommendations

**Your results are:**
- ‚úÖ Confidential (only you and authorized supervisors can see them)
- ‚úÖ Used to provide you with appropriate support
- ‚úÖ Tracked over time to monitor your progress

**Severity Levels Explained:**
- **Minimal:** You're doing well! Keep up your self-care routines
- **Mild:** Some symptoms present - consider talking to someone
- **Moderate:** Support recommended - speak with your supervisor
- **Moderately Severe/Severe:** Immediate support needed - you'll be contacted by a supervisor

#### üí™ Daily Affirmations

**What are they?**
Positive, encouraging messages delivered daily to boost your mental wellbeing.

**How they work:**
1. You receive a new affirmation each day via:
   - In-app notification (when you log in)
   - SMS (if enabled)
   - Email (if enabled)
2. Read and reflect on the message
3. Click "Like" if it resonated with you
4. Access your affirmation history anytime

**Affirmation Themes:**
- Self-esteem and confidence
- Resilience and coping
- Goal setting and achievement
- Mindfulness and gratitude
- Social connection
- Hope and positivity

#### üìÖ Events & Workshops

**What are they?**
Quarterly pillar events covering mental health topics, skills training, and community building.

**How to participate:**

1. **Browse Events:**
   - Go to "Events" section

   - Read event descriptions, dates, and locations

2. **Register for an Event:**
   - Click "Register" on any event

3. **Attend the Event:**

   - Participate actively
   - Network with other prevention advocates

4. **Post-Event:**

   - Share comments about your experience
   - Receive a certificate (for eligible events)

**Registration Status:**
- **Registered:** You've signed up
- **Confirmed:** Your spot is secured
- **Waitlisted:** Event is full, you're on the waiting list
- **Cancelled:** You've cancelled your registration

   - View status updates

**Tips for a Strong Application:**
- ‚úÖ Be specific about your project goals
- ‚úÖ Provide a realistic budget

- ‚úÖ Show community need and impact

## üß† Mental Health Features

UNDA provides comprehensive mental health support tools designed to promote wellbeing and early intervention.

### Mental Health Assessments

#### Assessment Types

**PHQ-9 (Patient Health Questionnaire - 9 items):**
- **Purpose:** Screens for depression
- **Questions:** 9 items about mood and behavior
- **Takes:** 2-3 minutes
- **Scoring:** 0-27 (higher = more severe)
- **Used for:** Regular monitoring and baseline assessment

**GAD-7 (Generalized Anxiety Disorder - 7 items):**
- **Purpose:** Screens for anxiety
- **Questions:** 7 items about worry and nervousness
- **Takes:** 2-3 minutes
- **Scoring:** 0-21 (higher = more severe)
- **Used for:** Anxiety symptom tracking

**PHQ-2 (Quick Depression Check):**
- **Purpose:** Brief depression screening
- **Questions:** 2 key items from PHQ-9
- **Takes:** 30 seconds
- **Used for:** Quick check-ins

**GAD-2 (Quick Anxiety Check):**
- **Purpose:** Brief anxiety screening
- **Questions:** 2 key items from GAD-7
- **Takes:** 30 seconds
- **Used for:** Quick check-ins
